SQL in Action

A look at real queries from the IPL analysis project — the kind of work I actually enjoy doing.

Query: Top run-scorers per season
-- Rank top batters per IPL season WITH season_runs AS ( SELECT season, batter, SUM(batsman_runs) AS total_runs, COUNT(DISTINCT match_id) AS matches FROM deliveries d JOIN matches m ON d.match_id = m.id GROUP BY season, batter ) SELECT season, batter, total_runs, DENSE_RANK() OVER ( PARTITION BY season ORDER BY total_runs DESC ) AS rank_in_season FROM season_runs WHERE rank_in_season <= 10;
Key Insights Found
  • 1
    Toss winner bats second 63% of the time — and teams chasing win more often in evening matches, pointing to dew factor impact.
  • 2
    Wankhede & Chinnaswamy have 20%+ higher avg scores than Chepauk — venue is a stronger predictor of match total than team batting order.
  • 3
    Death overs (16–20) account for 38% of total runs — a finding that validates team selection prioritising power hitters over classical anchors.
  • 4
    No-ball + wide rates spike in overs 15–17 — suggesting a measurable pressure-performance relationship in fielding during power phases.
